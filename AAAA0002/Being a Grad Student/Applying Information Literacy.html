Applying Information Literacy

Success in graduate school isn’t just about managing time or completing assignments—it’s about learning to think like a scholar. One essential skill is information literacy—the ability to locate, evaluate, and use scholarly sources effectively. Developing this skill will help you distinguish credible research from unreliable information, strengthen your writing, and prepare you for advanced projects like your capstone (or dissertation).

➡️ You’ll explore this topic in more detail in the next unit, Foundations of Scholarship. In that unit, you’ll practice strategies for asking research questions, analyzing scholarly articles, and using the APUS Trefry Library effectively.

The library is an indispensable partner in your journey to mastering information literacy, which is one of the most crucial skills for scholarly success. Building on your understanding of how to locate, evaluate, and use academic sources, the library offers targeted support and resources designed to refine these abilities. Whether you are searching for peer-reviewed articles, learning to distinguish credible research from unreliable information, or seeking guidance on advanced research methods, the library’s expert staff and comprehensive databases are ready to empower your growth.

Through tutorials and personalized assistance, you’ll gain firsthand experience in applying information literacy principles, ensuring your research is both thorough and credible. By fully engaging with the library’s offerings, you transform information literacy from theory into practice, positioning yourself for excellence in every academic endeavor.

Remember to review the Library module in its entirety and take full advantage of this valuable resource.
