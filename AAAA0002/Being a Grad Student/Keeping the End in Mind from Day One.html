Week 3 Being a Grad Student

Keeping the End in Mind from Day One

Even though the end-of-program requirement arrives only at the conclusion of your studies, keep it at the forefront of your mind as you progress through your courses. Planning for this final step shouldn’t wait until you are nearing graduation. By considering it early and often, you can make strategic choices throughout your program that will prepare you for a successful end-of-program experience. 

The final program requirement may be the most rigorous challenge of your academic career.

“Planning ahead” even applies to how you’ll complete your program as you near the finish line. As you close in on your last few courses, check the Student Handbook [webpage] to learn about the graduation process, and make sure you reach out to your faculty and advisors to discuss your end-of-program requirements. 

If your program offers multiple options, you may need to choose one of them early in your plan of study. You should investigate the available paths, such as a thesis, comprehensive exam, practicum, eportfolio, or creative project, and identify which best option aligns with your goals. Check your Academic Plan in ecampus to confirm what’s available. 

⚠️ Note: Many capstone courses run 16 weeks and must be taken after completing all other program requirements; They cannot be taken concurrently. 

A Note for Doctoral Students

The end-of-program requirement for doctoral students differs from the options available for master's students.

The master's program is likely to include options for the capstone project (thesis, etc.). Doctoral students, however, have to complete a dissertation. This capstone project focuses on researching a relevant question in the discipline and articulating its significance to professional practice.

Key differences in this process include the inclusion of a dissertation committee, a preparation phase, a proposal, and a defense.

Access the Dissertation Manual [PDF] to learn more about this end-of-program requirement, its expectations, the dissertation process, and more.

Identify Your Topic

As you move through your coursework, use assignments as stepping stones for your final thesis or capstone (or dissertation) project. When possible, choose topics that genuinely interest you.

Tip:Literature reviews or research papers can often serve as early groundwork.

One of the toughest parts of the research process is narrowing your focus. By identifying a topic early and doing some initial research, you’ll be much better prepared to refine your question and dive deeper when it is time to develop your final project.

➡️The Master's Studies [webpage]  and Doctoral Studies [webpage] pages in the Library provide valuable tools and guidance.

Know Your Degree Timeline

APUS is different from most brick-and-mortar colleges and universities in that students can potentially take courses year-round with no breaks and can register online, day or night, without the approval of an advisor or faculty member. Sometimes this model can be interpreted to mean that students can complete their degrees in a short amount of time while taking classes in any order; however, it is important to understand the time commitments of your schoolwork and your life to pace yourself. 

  • APUS offers new course sessions each month throughout the year. With this flexibility, plan courses in keeping with the requirements of your financial aid type and with awareness of your plans at home.  
  • Your funding type may dictate your schedule. For example, if you are using Federal Student Aid, your second semester will begin immediately after the first ends, potentially resulting in eight consecutive months of coursework. By comparison, students using VA benefits may have more flexibility in scheduling, but changes in enrollment can lead to a lapse in coverage or payments. If you rely on VA benefits for living expenses, be sure to plan your course schedule carefully to maintain consistent support.

Remember to contact the Financial Services [content page] team if you need assistance.
