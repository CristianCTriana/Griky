Week 3 Being a Grad Student

Sharpening Your Academic & Professional Skills

Graduate school is an excellent opportunity to develop both professional and academic skills that are important for career advancement. According to the Council of Graduate Schools, employers that hire individuals with graduate degrees expect them to possess several key skill sets in addition to advanced content knowledge.

If you have the chance to write a paper for publication, give a conference presentation, or lead a team, seize it! These experiences help build your resume while sharpening the skills employers value most.

Remember, too, that faculty are often looking for students to collaborate with on research, presentations, and academic projects.

By showing initiative and engaging with your instructors, you may unlock opportunities to co-author work, assist with research, or contribute to scholarly events. These collaborations can be transformative, enhancing your academic profile and opening doors to future career paths.
