Succeeding in Your Courses

Navigating your courses requires proactive engagement, strategic planning, and consistent communication. At APUS, your daily and weekly responsibilities closely mirror those in traditional institutions, but are tailored for the flexibility of an online, asynchronous format.

Graduate courses are reading-intensive and task-driven, so you should begin each week with a clear strategy. Start by reviewing the week‚Äôs assignments, discussion prompts, papers, quizzes, or projects. These tasks reveal what your instructor wants you to focus on, helping you prioritize the most relevant readings and concepts. 

Before diving into the materials, try the ‚Äútask-first‚Äù method: read the assessment instructions first, then preview the readings with those goals in mind. This purposeful approach helps filter out distractions and allows you to concentrate on what matters most. It is a simple shift in preparation that can dramatically improve comprehension and retention. 

To stay ahead, dedicate 10‚Äì15 minutes at the start of each week to scan your course content and build a plan. Use a calendar or planner to block out time for each task, adjusting the time blocks based on the week‚Äôs workload. This habit will keep you organized, but not overwhelmed, by setting the tone for confident, focused learning.

üí¨Active Participation and Communication

Staying engaged with your course community is crucial for success. 

  • Weekly Discussion Forums: Participation in discussions is required in most courses. The Discussion forums serve as a space for in-depth dialogue with instructors and classmates around weekly content. 
  • 1:1 Chat with Prof: This dedicated space allows you to send private messages to your instructor. Learn more about the 1:1 Chat with Prof in the Success Center [webpage]. 
  • Have a Question? If the question is course-related and might benefit others, post it in the open Q&A chat. For private matters, use the 1:1 Chat with Prof. 
  • Direct Messaging: Use the in-course instant messaging feature to communicate with your peers.

üó£ Connecting through Office Hours

In APUS's mostly asynchronous online environment, live office hours are an invaluable resource for academic and professional growth. These sessions enable you to ask questions about assignments, and they offer opportunities to build relationships with your instructors, gain deeper insights into course materials, and explore research or career interests. 

Faculty are required to be available to students, even if formal office hours are not posted. Don‚Äôt wait for an invitation. Reach out and schedule time to connect. These conversations can lead to mentorship, letters of recommendation, and even collaboration on academic projects or presentations. The more your instructors know about your goals and interests, the better they can support your journey. 

Tip: Treat office hours like networking appointments. Come prepared with questions, share your academic interests, and be open to feedback. Whether you are seeking clarification or exploring future opportunities, these meetings can be a turning point in your graduate experience.

‚úîÔ∏èMonitoring Grades and Feedback

It is important to always review and apply the feedback given to you by your instructors. Additionally, you should monitor your grades and course progress to ensure you are on track. The Navigating the Grades Tool [video] will help with this process.

üëçStaying Engaged and Committed

Logging in regularly, submitting assignments on time, and participating in discussions will all contribute to your academic success. In fact, consistent engagement is strongly linked to higher graduation rates. Staying enrolled and avoiding unnecessary withdrawals is also important, as these notations appear on your graduate transcript and may raise questions for future employers.

üÜòSeeking Help Early

If you encounter challenges or start to fall behind, reach out to your instructor early. Do not wait until you are completely overwhelmed. Proactive communication can help you create a plan to get back on track. Organization, communication, and consistency are key to academic success. 

By staying engaged, planning ahead, and using the tools and resources available to you, you‚Äôll be well positioned to thrive in your courses and move confidently toward your goals.
