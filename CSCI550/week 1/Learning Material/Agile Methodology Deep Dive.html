2.1 Core Agile Principles and Values

Agile methodology is guided by a set of principles and values that emphasize collaboration, customer satisfaction, and adaptability. The Agile Manifesto (Agile Manifesto, 2001) highlights four key values:

Agile prioritizes human interaction and teamwork over strict processes and tools. Collaboration between team members is crucial for achieving project goals effectively. By focusing on individuals and their interactions, Agile fosters a culture of open communication and mutual trust, which enhances productivity and innovation.

Agile values functional software that delivers value to the customer over extensive documentation. While documentation is important, the focus is on delivering tangible results that meet customer needs. Agile encourages teams to create “just enough” documentation to support development and future maintenance without delaying the delivery of working software.

Agile emphasizes close collaboration with the customer throughout the development process, ensuring that the final product meets their needs. Regular feedback helps refine the project and allows teams to adapt to changing requirements. This ongoing collaboration builds a strong relationship between the development team and the customer, resulting in a product that better aligns with the customer’s expectations.

Agile embraces change as a natural part of the development process. Instead of rigidly adhering to a plan, Agile encourages flexibility to adapt to evolving customer requirements. By welcoming changes, even late in the development process, Agile ensures that the software remains relevant and valuable to the customer.

In addition to these values, the 12 Agile Principles emphasize early and continuous delivery of valuable software, welcoming changing requirements, delivering frequently, and building projects around motivated individuals (Sabharwal et al., 2021).

Twelve Principles

Focus on Customer Satisfaction

Deliver value by understanding and meeting customer needs through frequent feedback and flexible offerings.

Welcome Change

Embrace change at any stage of the product's lifecycle to remain competitive and responsive to customer demands.

Deliver Frequently

Release working software in short cycles (e.g., MVPs) to gather feedback and ensure alignment with customer expectations.

Work Together

Break silos by fostering collaboration among business and IT teams to achieve shared goals and deliver value.

Establish a Collaborative Approach

Promote transparency, trust, and accountability across all teams to enhance communication and teamwork.

Deliver Working Software

Ensure frequent delivery of functional MVPs that provide value, meet requirements, and evolve with customer feedback.

Sustainable Development

Maintain a steady pace of delivery with a healthy work environment, ensuring that teams are not overburdened.

Maintain Simplicity

Focus on core deliverables that provide value, avoiding unnecessary complexity or unneeded features.

Reflect and Adjust

Regularly review and adapt processes through retrospectives to continuously improve and address gaps.

Self-Organizing Teams

Empower teams with ownership and accountability to make decisions and deliver value efficiently.

Continuous Feedback

Collect and act on customer feedback early and often to align with expectations and improve the product.

Trust and Support

Foster a culture of trust and empower teams with the tools, skills, and autonomy to make decisions and innovate.

These principles collectively guide Agile teams in delivering high-quality software that aligns with stakeholder expectations while maintaining adaptability.

Agile is not a single methodology but a collection of frameworks that share common principles. The most popular Agile frameworks include Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP).

Scrum: Scrum is one of the most widely used Agile frameworks, characterized by its use of short development cycles called sprints, which typically last two to four weeks. Scrum teams are cross-functional, meaning they include members with diverse skill sets who can contribute to the project’s different aspects. Scrum defines specific roles, such as the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and the Development Team. The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value of the product by prioritizing features and ensuring that the team understands the requirements. The Scrum Master ensures that the team follows Agile practices and helps remove any obstacles that may hinder progress. The Development Team works collaboratively to complete the tasks defined in each sprint (Sabharwal et al., 2021). Scrum’s focus on time-boxed sprints and regular reviews helps teams stay on track and continuously improve their processes.

Kanban: Kanban is another Agile framework that emphasizes visualizing the workflow and managing work in progress (WIP) limits. The Kanban Board is a central component, where tasks are represented as cards that move across columns, typically labeled “To Do,” “In Progress,” and “Done.” Kanban’s main goal is to optimize the flow of work and ensure that the team is not overwhelmed by taking on too much at once. Unlike Scrum, Kanban does not have predefined iterations; instead, it focuses on continuous delivery and process improvement (Hooda et al., 2023). By limiting WIP, Kanban helps teams maintain a steady pace and avoid bottlenecks, making it particularly effective for environments with unpredictable workloads or frequent changes.

Extreme Programming (XP): Extreme Programming (XP) is an Agile framework that places a strong emphasis on technical practices, such as Test-Driven Development (TDD), pair programming, and continuous integration. XP aims to improve software quality and responsiveness to changing customer requirements. It encourages developers to write tests before coding and emphasizes frequent releases, ensuring that feedback is received early and often. XP is particularly beneficial in projects with frequently changing requirements or those requiring high-quality code (Sabharwal et al., 2021). By focusing on best practices for coding and testing, XP helps teams deliver robust software that meets customer expectations while maintaining flexibility.
