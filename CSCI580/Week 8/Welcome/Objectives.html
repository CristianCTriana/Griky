Week 8 Welcome

Objectives

  • LO-1.1: Describe the key principles underlying modern computer architecture design.
  • LO-1.2: Explain the trade-offs involved in architectural design choices.
  • LO-1.3: Analyze different instruction set architectures (RISC vs. CISC).
  • LO-2.1: Calculate key performance metrics.
  • LO-2.2: Identify performance bottlenecks in each architecture..
  • LO-3.1: Describe techniques for exploiting instruction-level parallelism.
  • LO-3.2: Explain the concept of vectorization and its application in SIMD (Single Instruction Multiple Data) architectures.
  • LO-3.3: Discuss techniques for exploiting thread-level parallelism.
  • LO-3.4: Describe the challenges and solutions associated with exploiting parallelism in memory systems.
  • LO-4.1: Explain the sources of power consumption in modern computer architectures.
  • LO-4.2: Describe techniques for reducing power consumption at various levels.
  • LO-4.3: Evaluate the trade-offs between power efficiency and performance.
  • LO-4.4: Discuss the challenges and opportunities associated with designing low-power architectures.
  • LO-5.1: Describe the architecture and programming model of Graphics Processing Units (GPUs).
  • LO-5.2: Identify applications that are well-suited for GPU acceleration.
  • LO-5.3: Develop algorithms for execution on GPUs using CUDA or OpenCL.
  • LO-5.4: Discuss the role of specialized accelerators.
  • LO-6.1: Identify emerging trends in computer architecture.
  • LO-6.2: Discuss the impact of emerging trends on future computer systems and applications.
  • LO-6.3: Analyze the challenges and opportunities associated with designing and implementation.
  • LO-6.4: Examine the ethical and societal implications of emerging computer architectures.
  • LO-7.1: Analyze architectural design decisions using quantitative and qualitative metrics.
  • LO-7.2: Evaluate competing architectural solutions based on performance, power, and cost trade-offs
  • LO-7.3: Create evidence-based recommendations for architectural choices in complex computing scenarios.
