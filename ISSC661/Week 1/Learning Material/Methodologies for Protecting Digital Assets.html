Methodologies for Protecting Digital Assets

Protecting digital assets begins with a robust risk management framework that identifies vulnerabilities that exist and applies standardized controls as a way to mitigate those risks. Such frameworks ensure consistency and regulatory compliance while enabling organizations to prioritize investments through repeatable processes that test high-risk areas and minimize exposure (Death, 2023). Some examples of industry-recognized frameworks are:

  • ISO/IEC 27001
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework
  • PCI DSS

Protecting digital assets requires a layered approach, often described as “defense in depth.” One critical layer is access control. Organizations should ensure that system users have only the necessary permissions to perform their job (Death, 2023).

Access control is strengthened by role-based access (RBAC) and multi-factor authentication (MFA). Regardless of strategy, rigorous monitoring is crucial in addition to deploying these tools in order to adapt to evolving threats.

In addition to technical measures that an organization can take, operational practices play a critical role in protecting digital assets. These practices include regular data backups, software updates, password policies, and incident response planning, to name a few. Employee training is important due to insiders often being the greatest threat, whether intentional or the result of human error. Combining technical controls and competent operational practices, organizations can reduce vulnerabilities to cyber threats.
