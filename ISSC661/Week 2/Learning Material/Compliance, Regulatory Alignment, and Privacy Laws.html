Week 2 Organizational, Legal, and Ethical Dimensions

Compliance, Regulatory Alignment, and Privacy Laws

It is challenging for companies to operate in multiple jurisdictions due to various industry standards and data protection laws (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Those standards and regulations include
General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS)

To ensure compliance, organizations conduct audits, maintain documentation, and train employees on security policies. Protocols are needed for breaches, and so are incident response plans.

While organizations are trying to avoid costly penalties that come with non-compliance, it is also important that they build trust with customers and other stakeholders (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Compliance and ethics share a symbiotic relationship, reflecting a commitment to responsible data stewardship.

By integrating compliance into governance and risk management, organizations can create a holistic cybersecurity strategy that supports legal conformity and ethical integrity (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Privacy regulations, such as the GDPR and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), mandate data protection, transparency, and user rights (Edwards & Weaver, 2024). Privacy regulations are often enforced through encryption, access controls, and secure data storage. Privacy-by-design emphasizes privacy considerations at the onset and ensures that configuration is influenced by privacy and not treated as a separate domain (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Data minimization, purpose limitation, and user consent are important considerations and guide the design. Through the focus on privacy, organizations can protect user rights, comply with regulations, and foster ethical digital networks, thus building customer confidence.
