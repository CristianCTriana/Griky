Week 2 Organizational, Legal, and Ethical Dimensions

EU Law, AI, and Data Protection Challenges

Nash et al. (2024) provided several case study examples illustrating the legal complexities of reconciling data protection, AI, and cybersecurity under EU law. They noted that cybersecurity firms use personal information to provide cybersecurity services or to create cybersecurity tools and services (Nash et al., 2024).

Due to the protection of personal information in the EU, it might be challenging for firms that provide AI-based tools and services to do so for EU-based companies, given the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which strongly restricts their use.

Legislation will need to evolve to account for new risks, such as:

Algorithmic bias
Automated decision-making
Data re-identification

Transparency is critical, as is integrating legal, technical, and ethical perspectives to ensure compliance.
