Risk Management in a Dynamic Threat Landscape

Risk management is an essential part of a cybersecurity strategy.

A risk lifecycle should include: (Edwards & Weaver, 2024)


  • identification
  • analysis
  • evaluation
  • treatment
  • monitoring

Using qualitative and quantitative methods, organizations should assess the likelihood and impact of potential threats. In order to prioritize risk, tools can be used to help do so, and include heat maps, risk matrices, and scenario analysis. Risk tolerance should be clearly defined in order to guide better decision-making, and organizations must be agile so that they can respond quickly to emerging threats. Risk management involves stakeholder engagement, so that risks are communicated and understood across departments (Edwards & Weaver, 2024). Continuous monitoring and reassessment are needed, as the threat landscape changes.


"By implementing risk management in daily operations, organizations can make informed decisions, allocate resources effectively, and maintain resiliency (Edwards & Weaver, 2024)."
