Blockchain Fundamentals and Cybersecurity Challenges

Blockchain is a decentralized ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers. Its core attributes of immutability, transparency, and consensus make it a powerful tool for enhancing cybersecurity. Cybersecurity challenges today include data breaches, ransomware, identity theft, and advanced persistent threats. These attacks exploit weaknesses in centralized architectures and outdated protocols. Blockchain counters these vulnerabilities by distributing data across nodes, making unauthorized alterations nearly impossible without consensus (Barman et al., 2025).

Use cases include the following:

Blockchain enables decentralized identifiers (DIDs), allowing users to control their digital identities without relying on centralized authorities. This reduces the risk of identity theft and enhances privacy. For secure communications, blockchain can facilitate encrypted messaging systems where message integrity is verified through hash functions and timestamps.

By logging system events on a blockchain, organizations can detect anomalies and trace malicious activities. This immutable audit trail supports forensic investigations and compliance with regulatory standards (Barman et al., 2025).

By recording every transaction and movement of goods on a blockchain, companies can ensure authenticity and traceability, reducing the risk of counterfeit products and tampering.

Blockchain can provide transparent, tamper-proof records of votes, enhancing trust in electoral processes.

Blockchain can protect patient records while enabling authorized sharing across institutions.

Blockchain can provide decentralized authentication and firmware updates, reducing the risk of botnet attacks and data leaks.
