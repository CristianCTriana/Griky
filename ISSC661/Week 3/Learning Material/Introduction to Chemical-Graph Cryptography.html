Introduction to Chemical-Graph Cryptography

Chemical-graph cryptography is a novel cryptographic technique that combines chemical structures and graph theory. This interdisciplinary approach draws inspiration from molecular biology, where complex chemical compounds encode information in stable, intricate patterns (Barman et al., 2025).

Chemical graphs, representations of molecules as nodes and bonds, are used as the basis for encryption. Each chemical structure corresponds to a unique graph, which can be mathematically manipulated to encode and decode data.

This method introduces a new layer of complexity, making it difficult for adversaries to reverse-engineer the encryption without knowledge of the underlying chemical model. Unlike traditional cryptography, which relies on numerical keys and algorithms, chemical-graph cryptography uses molecular templates and graph transformations (Barman et al., 2025).

This approach is particularly suited for environments where biological data or chemical processes are integral, such as:

Bioinformatics
Pharmaceutical Research

This method is particularly promising for environments where traditional cryptographic keys are vulnerable or impractical. For instance, in biological systems or embedded devices, chemical-graph encryption offers a lightweight yet secure alternative.
