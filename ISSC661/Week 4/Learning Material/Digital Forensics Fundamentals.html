Digital Forensics Fundamentals

Digital forensics is the backbone of incident analysis. It is the collection, preservation, and analysis of digital evidence (Johansen, 2025). Key principles include:

Maintaining the chain of custody – documenting evidence handling to ensure the integrity of the evidence

Ensuring data integrity – data that is accurate and complete

Using validated tools – tools that have been tested and found to be accurate

Several forensic techniques can be used, such as disk imaging, file carving, and timeline analysis.

Evidence admissibility should always be considered throughout the process, as determining what happened is not the only factor; it is also about prosecution (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Organizations should invest in forensic capabilities as they not only support incident response but also threat intelligence (Goldschmidt, 2025).
