Week 6 International Standards, Frameworks, and Security Acts

International Cybersecurity Laws and Regulations

The global nature of digital threats necessitates cross-border cooperation and collaboration in legal frameworks. Multinational organizations must assess where their data flows, who their users are, and which laws apply.

The European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is the most prominent example, setting a high bar for data protection and privacy. GDPR applies to any organization processing personal data of EU residents, regardless of location.

It mandates lawful processing, data minimization, breach notification, and data subject rights (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

Non-compliance can result in fines up to €20 million.

Other countries have followed suit.

Brazil, South Korea, Canada

Brazil’s General Protection Law (LGPD), South Korea’s Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), and Canada’s Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) reflect similar principles.

China

China’s Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) emphasizes consent, data localization, and government oversight (Edwards & Weaver, 2024).

These laws vary in scope and enforcement but share a commitment to protecting personal data.
