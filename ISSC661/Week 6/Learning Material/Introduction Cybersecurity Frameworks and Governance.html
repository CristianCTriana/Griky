As digital threats grow in complexity and frequency, the need for structured approaches to managing cyber risks becomes paramount. Cybersecurity frameworks and models highlight best practices that guide organizations in securing their digital assets (Chourasia et al., 2024).

Frameworks such as NIST, ISO/IEC 27001, and COBIT offer structured methodologies for identifying, mitigating, and responding to cyber threats.

These models emphasize:

Risk Assessment
Continuous Monitoring
Incident Response

Governance, on the other hand, ensures that cybersecurity aligns with organizational objectives and legal mandates.

Together, frameworks and regulations form a dual shield—technical and legal—that protects data integrity, confidentiality, and availability.
