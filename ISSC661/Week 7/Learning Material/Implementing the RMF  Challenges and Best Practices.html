Implementing the RMF: Challenges and Best Practices

Implementing the RMF requires careful planning, cross-functional collaboration, and a clear understanding of organizational priorities. Several common challenges to implement the RMF include:

  • Resource constraints
  • Lack of stakeholder engagement
  • Difficulty translating technical risks into business terms

(Edwards & Weaver, 2024). Overcoming these hurdles demands a strategic approach.

One best practice is to align RMF activities with existing governance structures. For example, integrating the RMF with project management and procurement processes ensures that security is considered early and consistently.

Another is to use automation tools for control assessment and monitoring, which can reduce manual effort and improve accuracy.

Training and awareness programs are also critical, as they help stakeholders understand their roles and responsibilities within the RMF.

Not all controls are equally relevant, and risk tolerance varies across industries. A financial institution may prioritize data integrity, while a healthcare provider may focus on confidentiality. By customizing control selection and assessment criteria, organizations can optimize their security investments.
