Introduction: Cybersecurity Frameworks and Risk Management

Cybersecurity governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) are foundational pillars in managing digital threats and ensuring organizational resilience. Frameworks such as the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF), the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF), and NIST SP 800-53 provide structured approaches to identifying, assessing, and mitigating cyber risks.

These frameworks are not merely technical checklists; they are strategic tools that align cybersecurity with business objectives, regulatory requirements, and operational realities.

The NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) is a structured process developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology to guide organizations in managing information security and privacy risks. Although it is most often used by federal agencies and contractors, it is increasingly being adopted by the private sector.

The RMF consists of seven steps:

Prepare

Categorize

Select

Implement

Assess

Authorize

Monitor

Each step is designed to ensure that security and privacy considerations are integrated throughout the system life cycle. Its integration with enterprise risk management (ERM) frameworks allows cybersecurity to be treated as a business risk rather than a technical issue.

The RMF’s emphasis on accountability, documentation, and continuous monitoring makes it a cornerstone of effective cybersecurity governance.
