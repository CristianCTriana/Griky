Week 8 Integration and Future Directions

Ethical and Governance Challenges of GenAI

Although there are positive uses, the integration of GenAI into cybersecurity raises complex ethical questions. Because GenAI can generate content that mimics human behavior or fabricates realistic scenarios, it risks amplifying bias, misinformation, and manipulation if not properly governed (Islam, 2024).

Data Provenance

GenAI models often train on vast datasets that may include sensitive or proprietary information. Without clear guidelines on data sourcing and consent, organizations risk violating privacy laws or ethical norms (Islam, 2024).

Algorithmic Accountability

GenAI systems can make autonomous decisions in high-stakes environments, such as blocking access or flagging users. If these decisions are incorrect or biased, they can cause harm.

Explainable AI (XAI) techniques that allow stakeholders to understand and audit GenAI outputs should be implemented.

Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration

Cybersecurity is no longer just a technical domain; it requires ethical foresight and societal engagement. GenAI must be developed with safeguards that reflect human values and democratic principles, and that include collaboration among technologists, ethicists, and policymakers.
