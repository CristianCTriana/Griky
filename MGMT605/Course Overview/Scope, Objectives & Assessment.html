MGMT605
 Course Overview

Scope, Learning Objectives, and Assessment

| Course Scope

This course is divided into 8 weeks and is organized to give students the history, theories, and working concepts of leadership. This will be accomplished by the use of textbook readings, practical exercises, and online interaction and inquiry through the extensive use of discussion Forum participation.

| Course & Learning Objectives

Select each Course Objective to see the associated Learning Objectives and Assessments.

Learning Objectives

  • LO-1.1: Analyze the conceptual distinctions between leadership and management by synthesizing scholarly frameworks and evaluating their implications for organizational effectiveness.
  • LO-1.2: Evaluate theoretical perspectives on leadership and management, including transformational, transactional, and servant leadership, to determine their relevance in shaping organizational roles and culture. 
  • LO-1.3: Assess the influence of organizational norms, values, and power dynamics on the behavior and decision-making of leaders and managers within complex systems. 
  • LO-1.4: Design evidence-based strategies that integrate leadership and management practices to promote ethical governance, psychological safety, and long-term organizational health. 

Assessments

  • Week 1 - Discussion 
  • Week 2 - Assignment: Bridging the Gap: Leadership and Management in Organizational Contexts

| Program Learning Outcomes

Program Learning Outcomes (PLO) serve as a roadmap for educational success and guide the courses that you take.

This course supports the following PLOs:

  • Conduct business problem focused management research, including collection and analysis of data, and communication of findings by using qualitative and quantitative research methods.
  • Develop a strategic business plan applying the theories and practices of management and planning for a particular organizational setting.
  • Develop an analytical framework using a variety of approaches to apply leadership and management theory and practice to address the challenges of global organizations in this century.
  • Examine complex organizations from a level of analysis perspective, including the individual, interpersonal, group, and organizational level perspectives.
  • Evaluate management issues in the context of ethical, technological, structural, cultural, human and environmental factors.
