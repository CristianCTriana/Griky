Leadership

Dynamic Leadership Across Contexts

Leadership is not static, it must evolve with context. Kammoe (2025) introduces the concept of dynamic leadership, emphasizing the need for leaders to adapt their approach based on situational variables such as team composition, organizational culture, external pressures, and crisis scenarios.

A leader who thrives in a startup may struggle in a bureaucratic institution unless they adjust their style. Contextual intelligence—the ability to read the environment and respond appropriately—is essential for effective leadership.
