Models for Leading Change

Agreement and Certainty Matrix

The Agreement and Certainty Matrix is a decision-making framework developed by Ralph D. Stacey often used in leadership and organizational contexts to help leaders understand the nature of the challenges they face and choose appropriate strategies.

It maps situations based on two dimensions:

  • Refers to how much consensus exists among stakeholders about what should be done.
  • Ranges from high agreement (clear goals, shared values) to low agreement (conflicting interests, unclear objectives).
  • Refers to how predictable the outcomes are and how well-understood the cause-and-effect relationships are
  • Ranges from high certainty (clear data, proven methods) to low certainty (complex, ambiguous, or novel situations).
Four Zones in the Matrix
Zone Description Leadership Implications
Simple High agreement, high certainty Use best practices and standard procedures.
Complicated High agreement, low certainty Use expert analysis and strategic planning.
Complex Low agreement, low certainty Encourage experimentation, dialogue, and adaptive leadership.
Chaotic Low agreement, high uncertainty Act quickly to stabilize, then move toward complexity or simplicity.

Why It’s Useful in Leadership

  • Helps leaders diagnose the nature of a problem.

  • Guides them in choosing the appropriate leadership style or decision-making approach.

  • Encourages adaptive thinking in dynamic environments.
