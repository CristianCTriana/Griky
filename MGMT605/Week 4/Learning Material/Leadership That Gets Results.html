Daniel Goleman popularized emotional intelligence and used insights from his work in this area to identify six styles of leadership.
Description Phrase Characteristics Pros and Cons
Coercive (Commanding) This style demands immediate compliance. “Do what I tell you.”
  • Focuses on control and discipline.
  • Often used in crisis situations or when turnaround is needed.

Pros: Effective in emergencies or with problem employees.

Cons: Can stifle creativity and demoralize teams if overused.

Authoritative (Visionary) Mobilizes people toward a vision. “Come with me.”
  • Provides clear direction and purpose.
  • Encourages autonomy in achieving goals.

Pros: Inspires and motivates; builds commitment.

Cons: Less effective when team members are more experienced than the leader.

Affiliative Creates emotional bonds and harmony. “People come first.”
  • Focuses on team cohesion and morale.
  • Values emotional needs and relationships.

Pros: Builds trust and loyalty.

Cons: May avoid confronting poor performance or conflict.

Democratic Forges consensus through participation. “What do you think?”
  • Encourages collaboration and input.
  • Builds commitment through involvement.

Pros: Boosts morale and innovation.

Cons: Can slow decision-making and frustrate in urgent situations.

Pacesetting Sets high standards for performance. “Do as I do, now.”
  • Leads by example.
  • Expects excellence and self-direction.

Pros: Works well with highly motivated and competent teams.

Cons: Can overwhelm and demoralize; may neglect emotional needs.

Coaching Develops people for the future. “Try this.”
  • Focuses on personal development.
  • Encourages learning and growth.

Pros: Builds long-term strengths and capabilities.

Cons: Requires time and patience; less effective if employees are resistant.
