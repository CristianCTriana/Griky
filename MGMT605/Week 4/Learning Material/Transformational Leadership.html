Models for Leading Change

Transformational Leadership

Beverley Alimo-Metcalfe’s research into leadership—particularly transformational leadership—offers a distinctive and inclusive perspective that contrasts sharply with traditional transactional models. Her work, especially through the development of the Transformational Leadership Questionnaire (TLQ), identifies several key factors that differentiate transformational leaders from transactional ones.

CKey Factors of Transformational Leadership (Alimo-Metcalfe)

  • Transformational leaders show authentic care for their team members’ personal and professional growth.
  • They understand and navigate complex stakeholder environments with tact and awareness.
  • They articulate a compelling vision and build networks to support it.
  • Focus on empowering others and nurturing future leaders.
  • Ethical behavior and transparency are central to their leadership style.
  • They are emotionally available and responsive to others’ needs.
  • They demonstrate strength and resilience in decision-making and adversity.
  • They foster inclusion and clarity in roles and responsibilities.
  • They promote innovation and strategic insight across the organization.
Transactional Leadership (Contrast)
  • Based on exchange and compliance.
  • Focuses on performance monitoring, reward/punishment, and short-term goals.
  • Less emphasis on emotional intelligence, vision, or personal development.
Alimo-Metcalfe’s model emphasizes “nearby” leadership—leaders who are accessible, inclusive, and collaborative, rather than distant or heroic. This approach is especially relevant in the public sector and diverse organizational contexts, where engagement and trust are critical.
