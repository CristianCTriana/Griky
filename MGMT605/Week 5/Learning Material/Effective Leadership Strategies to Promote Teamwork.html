Week 5
How Leadership Relates to Teamwork

Effective Leadership Strategies to Promote Teamwork

Learning Challenge

ⓘ This is not an assignment prompt, but rather an opportunity to reflect on how the week’s concepts apply to real workplace situations.

Below are a few of the elements of leadership strategy that can most highly affect teamwork.

A change in how decisions are made for a team can be useful in improving how the team works. In certain scenarios, leaders must make quick and decisive choices, or else they risk grave consequences to the business. Otherwise, democratic decisions that include all team members or team representatives can foster a sense of belonging and ownership among the team members. If they know their opinions and input are valued when decisions are being made, they will have a greater sense of responsibility for the ongoing team projects. This sense of responsibility to the goals of the team and to the other individual members is a necessary ingredient in teamwork.

Team members should receive accurate, well-thought-out feedback from their managers on a regular basis. Setting a level of accountability helps team members know that the team is serious about operating effectively. It also lets each department or individual know what areas they can improve to benefit the company and team. Without this knowledge, it becomes difficult for team members to know if they are helping or hurting the team. It also helps to set a level of trust that everyone is doing their part.

To produce the strongest results, teams should be oriented around the vision. This focus must come from the leader. As a leader, it’s important to bring up the vision in every discussion or meeting. How will our end goal be affected by this decision? Are these new changes beneficial to the vision?

Simple questions like these help the whole team to align themselves into the same mindset of placing customer needs above all else. Once mindsets become aligned, a sense of teamwork can easily develop as each member of the team is concentrated on meeting the same end needs: customer satisfaction.

Praising general team successes and individual successes can promote greater unity among team members. If members do not get any recognition when they put forth greater efforts, they can become discouraged and disenfranchised from the rest of the team. Leaders should give appropriate and deserved recognition to their team members. Remember that their success is your success as a leader!
