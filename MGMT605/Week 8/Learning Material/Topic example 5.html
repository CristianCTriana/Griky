Assessing Organizational Strategy

Topic name 5

Before an organization can develop its strategies and engage in strategic management, it must have a well-defined vision that its leaders and managers clearly understand. Vision can be defined as a statement that explains why an organization exists and where the organization is striving to go.

When an organization operates with a clear vision communicated to everyone involved, it will operate with a sense of purpose. Its leaders, managers, and employees will understand the direction the organization needs to go and why it wants to go there.

As Nickels et al. (2013) explain, a vision is critical because it provides direction, ensuring an organization’s members understand what they are trying to accomplish. Without a vision, an organization is likely to flounder, constantly changing directions because its members do not understand the goals they are seeking to reach.

Collins and Porras (2011) argue that a good vision is comprised of the following four components:

Core values
Refer to the principles that guide an organization as it carries out its work. For example, the Walt Disney Company has been built on the core values of imagination and wholesomeness.
Core purpose
Refers to an organization’s fundamental reason for existing. For example, the Walt Disney Company’s core purpose is to make people happy, motivating its employees to fulfill their tasks.
Once developed, these create the framework necessary to support an organization’s vision. This framework should remain unchanged, guiding and motivating an organization as it moves forward. This framework also provides the necessary foundation to develop an organization’s strategies and support its strategic management efforts.
