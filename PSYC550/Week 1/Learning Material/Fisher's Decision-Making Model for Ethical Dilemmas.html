Week 1 Introduction to Professional Ethics and Ethical Decision-Making Models

Fisher's Decision-Making Model for Ethical Dilemmas

The ethics code does not provide specific instructions for how to handle ethical dilemmas. It is the responsibility of the practitioner to have a framework for addressing ethical dilemmas.

Fisher (2023) proposes the following approach to ethical decision making:

  1. Identify the ethical problem.
  2. Identify the relevant ethical principles, standards, and guidelines.
  3. Identify the laws, regulations, and policies that are relevant to ethical planning and resolution of the dilemma. This includes the APA Ethics Code, federal/state/county law or policy, organizational policy, etc.
  4. Determine the stakeholdersâ€™ perspectives (who will be affected by and who will affect the outcome of the decision).
  5. Consider alternatives and identify and implement a plan. Identify each course of action and consider the consequences of each path. After careful consideration of the various courses of actions, the practitioner should choose the best course of action.
  6. Monitor and evaluate the plan to determine if changes need to be made.

Corey et al. (2020) recommend consultation with other professionals on how they might approach the ethical dilemma. Consultation should not be limited to those in the same field. If there are legal aspects to the dilemma, consultation with a lawyer is important.

Additionally, practitioners should maintain ongoing documentation of the decision-making process steps identified above.
