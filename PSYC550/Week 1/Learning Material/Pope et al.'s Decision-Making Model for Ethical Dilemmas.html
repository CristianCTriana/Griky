Week 1 Introduction to Professional Ethics and Ethical Decision-Making Models

Pope et al.'s Decision-Making Model for Ethical Dilemmas

Pope, Vasquez, Chavez-Duenas, and Adames (2021) propose a 17-step decision-making model for ethical dilemmas as follows:

  1. Identify the issue, dilemma, or concern clearly
  2. Consider who will be affected by the decision
  3. Identify the client
  4. Assess whether the psychologist possesses the competence for the situation or if there is someone else who would be able to do a better job
  5. Review relevant formal ethical standards
  6. Review relevant legal standards
  7. Review relevant research and theory
  8. Assess how personal biases may impact ethical judgment
  9. Examine social, cultural, and religious factors impacting the situation
  10. Consider consultation
  11. Develop alternative courses of action
  12. Consider the implications of each course of action
  13. Adopt the perspective of the individuals who will be affected by the decision
  14. Decide on a course of action
  15. Document the process and assess the results
  16. Assume personal responsibility for the consequences
  17. Considers implications for preparation, planning, and prevention

Although Pope et al. (2021) list documentation as one of the final steps, it is highly recommended that practitioners begin thorough documentation as soon as the ethical dilemma or concern is identified.
