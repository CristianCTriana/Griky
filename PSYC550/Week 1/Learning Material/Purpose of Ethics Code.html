Week 1 Introduction to Professional Ethics and Ethical Decision-Making Models

Purpose of Ethics Code

Fisher (2023) identified four purposes to the ethics code:

1. Establishing the integrity of the profession

The code reflects collective professional standards to guide individual psychological practice.

2. Education and professional socialization

The code establishes reasonable expectations for professional behavior in the field and guides individuals in the resolution of ethical standards.

3. Public Trust

The code provides the public with a set of expectations for professional encounters.

4. Enforcement Value

The code serves as a guide for practitioners on avoiding ethical complains and provides the public with a way to report ethical complaints.

Herlihy & Corey (2015) emphasize the ethics code as a means of fostering professional accountability, individual striving toward the highest ethical standards and encouraging colleagues to do the same. In addition, ethics codes promote improvements in practice.

The ethics code establishes guidelines for professional conduct but does not offer absolute guidance on how to handle every ethical dilemma. Pope and Vazquez (2016) remind us that ethics codes are critical, but insufficient in ethical responsibility.

Limitations in Ethics Codes

There are several limitations as noted in Herlihy and Corey (2015) and Pope and Vazquez (2016):

  • Ethics codes lack clarity and specificity in identifying ethical issues.
  • Knowledge of the codes does not guarantee ethical behavior.
  • Ethical codes may conflict with state laws or organizational standards.

Familiarity with ethical standards (what the guidelines suggest) and the community standard (what happens in practice) is essential. Knowing how other practitioners approach dilemmas is valuable information.
